Our Indigineous Animals

Indigenous animals are species that are native to a particular region or ecosystem and have naturally evolved and adapted to that specific environment over a long period. These animals play integral roles in their ecosystems, and their presence is often tied to the overall health and balance of the local environment.Sri Lanka, being an island nation with diverse ecosystems, is home to a variety of indigenous animals.Some of these species are found only in Sri Lanka and contribute to the country's unique biodiversity. Here are a few notable indigenous animals.

Sri Lanka, being an island surrounded by Indian Ocean is the major reason behind the existence of higher amount of endemic animal species which is also one of the highest rates of biological endemism in the world. Sri Lanka is home to about 21 species of endemic mammals, 26 species of birds, 113 species of reptiles, 112 species of amphibians, 50 species of fish and numerous insect species including 24 species of endemic butterflies such as Mycalesis rama and Elymnias singhala. Sri Lankan junglefowl, Purple-faced langur and Sri Lanka blue magpie are some of common endemic animals that can be observed. Distribution of these animals throughout the country vary according to the ecosystem diversity in Sri Lanka. Sinharaja Rain forest is an ecosystem with one of the highest rate of endemism being the only location where 21 out of 26 endemic bird species can be observed.

Sri Lankan Elephant

The Sri Lankan elephant is one of three subspecies of the Asian elephant, and it is the largest of them all. It is native to the island of Sri Lanka, where it is mostly restricted to the dry zone in the north, east, and southeast of the country. This elephant can be spotted in Lunugamvehera National Park, Wilpattu National Park, Udawalawe National Park, Minneriya National Park, and Yala National Park. Conflicts with humans are on the rise because of urban expansion and permanent cultivation.

Sri lankan Jungle Fowl

The Sri Lankan junglefowl (Gallus lafayettii), also known as the Ceylon junglefowl, is a bird species native to Sri Lanka. It is the national bird of Sri Lanka and is closely related to the Red junglefowl (Gallus gallus), which is considered the wild ancestor of domestic chickens.It has the distinct honor of being the national bird of Sri Lanka and is depicted in postage stamps etc.The Sri Lankan junglefowl is culturally significant in Sri Lanka and is featured on the country's national emblem. It holds a symbolic value and is often associated with the nation's identity.

Sri Lankan Leopard

The Sri Lankan leopard (Panthera pardus kotiya), also known as the Ceylon leopard, is a subspecies of leopard native to Sri Lanka.Sri Lankan leopards have a striking appearance with a golden-yellow coat covered in distinctive dark spots and rosettes. They are generally smaller in size compared to other leopard subspecies. These leopards inhabit a variety of ecosystems ranging from dry arid zones to rainforests, including the central highlands of Sri Lanka. They are known to adapt to different altitudes and terrains.

Sri lankan Sloth Bear

The Sri Lankan sloth bear (Melursus ursinus inornatus) is a subspecies of the sloth bear found in Sri Lanka.Sloth bears, in general, have a shaggy black coat with a distinctive pale, V-shaped mark on their chest. They have a long, shaggy coat, long, curved claws, and a mobile snout adapted for sucking up insects.These bears inhabit a variety of habitats, including grasslands, scrublands, and forests. They are primarily found in the dry zone of Sri Lanka, but their range can extend into other ecosystems.

Sri lankan Slender Loris

The Sri Lankan slender loris (Loris tardigradus tardigradus) is a small, nocturnal primate native to Sri Lanka. It belongs to the genus Loris and is one of several species of slender lorises found in South Asia.Slender lorises are characterized by their slender bodies, large round eyes, and a distinct face. The Sri Lankan slender loris has a grey or brownish coat, large eyes, and a short snout. It has a long tail, which is usually shorter than its body.These animals are primarily found in the rainforests, montane forests, and dry-zone forests of Sri Lanka. They are arboreal, spending most of their lives in trees.

Sri Lankan blue Magpie

Sri Lanka Blue Magpie (Urocissa ornata), also known as the Ceylon Magpie is a striking bird with a long tail and vibrant blue plumage. It has a black mask around its eyes and a contrasting white patch on its wings. The tail is long and graduated, and the overall appearance is quite distinctive.These magpies are endemic to Sri Lanka and are usually found in the wet zone's dense primary and secondary forests. They inhabit both lowland and montane forests.
