Department Of Wildlife Conservation

The Department of Wildlife Conservation of Sri Lanka plays a pivotal role in the preservation and sustainable management of the country's rich biodiversity. Established with the primary objective of safeguarding the diverse flora and fauna, the department is tasked with implementing conservation policies, managing national parks and reserves, and promoting awareness about wildlife conservation. Operating under the Ministry of Environment, the Department of Wildlife Conservation oversees a network of protected areas, including national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and nature reserves.

These areas serve as habitats for a myriad of species, including many that are endemic to Sri Lanka. The department engages in scientific research, habitat restoration, and wildlife monitoring to ensure the well-being of the country's unique ecosystems. Through its dedicated efforts, it aims not only to conserve endangered species but also to maintain the ecological balance and promote sustainable coexistence between wildlife and human communities.
