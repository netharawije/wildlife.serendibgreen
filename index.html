Activities

  1. Safari Tours
    • Yala National Park: Known for its leopard population, Yala is one of the most popular national parks in Sri Lanka. You can also spot elephants, sloth bears, and a variety of bird species.
    • Wilpattu National Park: Famous for its natural lakes (villus) and diverse wildlife, including leopards, elephants, and water birds.
  2. Bird Watching:
    • Sinharaja Forest Reserve: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Sinharaja is a biodiversity hotspot with numerous bird species, including the Sri Lanka Blue Magpie.
    • Bundala National Park: A haven for birdwatchers, Bundala is home to migratory birds, flamingos, and various waterfowl.
  3. Whale Watching
    • Mirissa: Experience whale and dolphin watching off the southern coast of Sri Lanka. Species include blue whales, sperm whales, and various dolphin species.
  4. Elephant Safaris
    • Minneriya National Park: You can witness large herds of elephants congregating near the Minneriya Tank.
    • Udawalawe National Park: Known for its elephant population, Udawalawe offers opportunities to observe these majestic animals in their natural habitat.
  5. Turtle Watching
    • Rekawa Beach: This beach, located near Tangalle, is a nesting site for sea turtles. You can witness turtle hatchings during the nesting season.
